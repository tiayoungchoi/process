Corporal of Korean Marine Base Found Dead

from Gunshot

A Korean corporal who had embarked on a maritime search mission was found dead from a gunshot wound to the head. Military investigators and the police are focusing on the possibility that the corporal may have taken his own life, while not ruling out the chance of an accidental discharge.

According to the Marine Corps, on the morning of the 13th, at a unit on Daechungdo in Ongjin County, Incheon, Corporal A was found dead. A unit officer discovered Corporal A collapsed in the driver’s seat of a military truck with a gunshot wound to the forehead and reported it immediately. Emergency treatment was administered, but he was officially pronounced dead at 9:01 a.m. on the 13th.

An autopsy revealed that Corporal A had been shot by a personal rifle. Some speculated that he may have died from an accidental gun discharge, but it is believed that he likely took his own life due to personal distress. With the preliminary cause of death established, the Marine Corps plans to hold a funeral on the 15th with the consent of the family. However, since the possibility of an accidental discharge cannot be completely ruled out, a thorough investigation into the exact cause will continue even after the funeral.

In addition to Corporal A, there have been a series of recent incidents in the military that were concluded as suicides or suspected suicides, highlighting the urgent need for preventive measures. On the 2nd, Captain B, who served as an instructor at the Army’s 3rd Officer School in Yeongcheon, Gyeongbuk, took his own life using firearm. Prior to that, on the 23rd of last month, Sergeant C of the Army did the same. Military sources say there are numerous similar incidents that have not been publicly disclosed.

On the 5th, Defense Minister An Gyu-baek chaired a key commanders’ meeting on military discipline and emphasized, “We will actively implement institutional measures, such as tailored suicide prevention programs for each field, to do our utmost in preventing accidents.”
